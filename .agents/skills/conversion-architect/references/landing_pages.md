# Landing Page Copywriting Guide

Landing pages have one job: Conversion. Every element on the page should lead the user toward the goal.

## Key Elements of a High-Converting Landing Page

### 1. The Hero Headline
- **The Hook**: Must capture attention in < 3 seconds.
- **The Value Prop**: What's in it for them?
- **Clarity > Cleverness**: It's better to be clear about what you offer than to be witty and confusing.

### 2. The Sub-headline
- Expands on the headline.
- Provides context and further justification.
- Sets the tone for the rest of the page.

### 3. Benefit Bullets
- Focus on the "So what?"
- Feature: "10GB Storage" -> Benefit: "Keep all your memories safe without ever running out of space."

### 4. Social Proof
- Testimonials should be specific and outcome-oriented.
- Logos, trust badges, and "as seen in" sections.

### 5. The Lead Magnet / Offer
- Must be irresistibly valuable.
- Low barrier to entry.

### 6. The Call to Action (CTA)
- Use action-oriented language.
- "Get My Free Guide" instead of "Submit".
- Create visual contrast with the rest of the page.

## Conversion Psychology Tips

- **Reciprocity**: Give value before asking for it.
- **Authority**: Demonstrate why you (or the client) are an expert.
- **Liking**: Use a relatable voice and tone.
- **Social Proof**: People follow the lead of similar others.
- **Scarcity/Urgency**: Deadlines or limited quantities increase desire.
- **Commitment & Consistency**: Start with small asks to lead to bigger ones.
