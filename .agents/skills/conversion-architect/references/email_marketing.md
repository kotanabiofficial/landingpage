# Email Marketing Copywriting Guide

Email is one of the most personal marketing channels. Success depends on the Subject Line (Open Rate) and the Body Copy (Click-Through Rate).

## The Anatomy of a High-Performing Email

### 1. The Subject Line
- **Curiosity Hook**: "I shouldn't be telling you this..."
- **Urgency/Scarcity**: "Final 4 hours (Ends at midnight)"
- **Direct Value**: "Your 20% Discount Inside"
- **Avoid Spam Triggers**: Don't use ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation points, or words like "CASH", "WIN", "FREE" in suspicious contexts.

### 2. The Preview Text (Snippet)
- Continue the thought from the subject line.
- Give a reason to click without giving it all away.

### 3. The Opening Line
- Address them by name (Personalization).
- Start with a "Yes" question or a relatable statement.

### 4. The Body Copy
- **Keep it Scannable**: Short paragraphs, bullet points.
- **The "So What?"**: Every sentence should lead to the next.
- **Storytelling**: Use anecdotes to build rapport before the pitch.

### 5. The Call to Action (CTA)
- Use clear buttons or underlined links.
- Repeat the CTA once in the middle and once at the end (or in a P.S.).

## Common Email Sequences

### 1. Welcome Sequence
- **Email 1**: The Delivery (Value/Lead Magnet) + Brand Intro.
- **Email 2**: The "Why" (Your Mission/Story).
- **Email 3**: The Proof (Case Studies/Testimonials).
- **Email 4**: The Pitch (Special Offer).

### 2. Abandoned Cart / Re-engagement
- **Reminder**: "Did you forget something?"
- **Incentive**: "Need a little help? Here's 10% off."
- **Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)**: "Your cart is about to expire."

### 3. The Newsletter / Nurture
- Focus 80% on Value/Education and 20% on Promotion.
- Maintain a consistent schedule (Weekly/Bi-weekly).

## Conversion Optimization for Email

- **Mobile First**: 60%+ of emails are read on mobile. Keep it narrow and readable.
- **P.S. Section**: People often scroll to the bottom. Use the P.S. for a final "Hail Mary" CTA or a summary of the offer.
- **Plain Text vs. HTML**: Plain text (or minimalist HTML) often feels more personal and has better deliverability.
