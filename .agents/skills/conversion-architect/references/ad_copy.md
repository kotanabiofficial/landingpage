# Ad Copywriting Guide

Advertising copy must be punchy, relevant, and platform-native. Each platform has its own "vibe" and technical constraints.

## Platform-Specific Strategies

### 1. Google Ads (Search)
- **Keyword Relevancy**: The headline MUST contain the primary keyword.
- **Dynamic Insertion**: Use `{KeyWord:Default Text}` where appropriate.
- **Benefits First**: "Save 30% Today" > "We Sell Shoes".
- **Path 1 & 2**: Use these fields to reinforce the CTA or value prop.
- **Extensions**: Always plan for Sitelinks, Callouts, and Structured Snippets.

### 2. Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)
- **The Hook (First 2 Lines)**: On mobile, only the first 2 lines of primary text are visible before "See More". Hook them immediately.
- **Instagram vs. Facebook**: IG is visual-first; keep text lighter. FB can handle longer-form "story" ads.
- **Emoji Usage**: Use emojis to break up text and guide the eye (e.g., ✅ 🚀 👉).
- **Threads Ads**: Treat like Twitter—witty, conversational, and timely.

### 3. TikTok Ads
- **TikTok Native**: Don't make ads, make TikToks. Use trending sounds and "creator-style" editing.
- **Captions**: Keep them short. The video does 90% of the work.
- **Strong Hook in Video**: The first 1.5 seconds are critical.
- **CTA**: Use TikTok's built-in buttons effectively.

### 4. WhatsApp Ads (Click-to-WhatsApp)
- **Immediate Value**: "Chat with us to get your custom plan."
- **Low Friction**: Focus on the ease of starting a conversation.
- **Auto-Reply Alignment**: Ensure the ad copy matches the initial automated message in WA.

## General Ad Copy Principles

- **One Single CTA**: Don't confuse the user. One ad, one goal.
- **Mirror the Landing Page**: The message on the ad must match the message on the page they land on (Message Match).
- **Test Everything**: Headlines, hooks, CTAs, and visual descriptions.
- **Speak to the "Me"**: Use "You" and "Your" to make it personal.
- **Address Objections**: "No Credit Card Required", "Free Shipping", "5-Star Rated".
