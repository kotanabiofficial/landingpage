# GEO: AI & Generative Engine Optimization Guide

Strategies for optimizing content for Search Generative Experience (SGE), LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude), and AI Answer Engines (Perplexity).

## Core GEO Strategies

### 1. The "Answer-First" Framework
- **Direct Responses**: Answer the user's likely question in the very first sentence.
- **Featured Snippet Bait**: Use a clear "definition" or "step-by-step" block at the top of the page.
- **Inverted Pyramid**: State the conclusion first, followed by supporting evidence and context.

### 2. Information Extraction Optimization
- **Structured Data**: Use JSON-LD (Schema.org) extensively. AI engines use this as a primary source of truth.
- **Tables & Lists**: AI models excel at extracting data from `<table>`, `<ul>`, and `<ol>` tags.
- **Semantic Clarity**: Use clear, unambiguous language. Avoid overly flowery prose that might confuse an LLM's entity extraction.

### 3. Authority & Attribution (Citations)
- **Authoritative Statements**: Use strong, fact-based declarations.
- **Cite Your Sources**: Linking to high-authority external data increases the "fact-score" of your content.
- **Entity Linking**: Connect your topic to well-known entities (people, places, organizations) to help AI engines place your content in the Knowledge Graph.

### 4. Natural Language FAQ (NLU)
- **Question-Based Headers**: Use H2/H3 tags that match real-world voice search or LLM prompts (e.g., "What are the requirements for...").
- **Concise Answers**: Keep FAQ answers between 40-60 words for maximum snippet potential.

## Checklist for AI Visibility
- [ ] Does the page provide a concise "TL;DR" or summary?
- [ ] Is the data presented in a structured format (List/Table)?
- [ ] Are facts supported by data points or external citations?
- [ ] Is the language clear and free of jargon that isn't semantically defined?
- [ ] Does the Schema.org markup accurately represent the page entities?
