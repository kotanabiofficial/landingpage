# A/B Testing & Experimentation Framework

A rigorous approach to testing and improving conversion rates.

## 1. Hypothesis Generation
Structure: "If we **[Change]**, then **[Metric]** will **[Increase/Decrease]** because **[Reason]**."
*Example*: "If we move the Trust Badges above the fold, then Conversion Rate will increase because users will trust the brand earlier in their journey."

## 2. Prioritization (PIE Framework)
- **Potential**: How much improvement can be made on this page?
- **Importance**: How valuable is the traffic to this page?
- **Ease**: How difficult is it to implement this test?
*Goal*: Start with high PIE scores.

## 3. Test Design
- **Single Variable**: Only test one major thing at a time (e.g., Headline vs. Headline) to ensure clear results.
- **Sample Size**: Ensure the test runs long enough to reach statistical significance (usually 95%+ confidence).
- **Traffic Split**: 50/50 split between Control (A) and Variant (B).

## 4. Analysis & Learning
- **Winners**: If B wins, implement it as the new "Control" and design a new test.
- **Losers**: If A wins, understand *why* B failed. A "failed" test is still a successful learning.
- **Inconclusive**: If no clear winner, consider if the change was too small or if the sample size was too low.

## Common Testing Ideas
- **Headlines**: Benefits vs. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO).
- **CTAs**: Color, Text ("Daftar Sekarang" vs. "Konsultasi Gratis"), and Placement.
- **Images**: Stock photos vs. Real high-quality photos of the product/service in use.
- **Social Proof**: Testimonials vs. Logos/Trust Badges.
- **Forms**: Single-step vs. Multi-step.
