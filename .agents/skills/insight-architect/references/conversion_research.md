# Conversion Research & Friction Analysis

Identifying the "Where" and "Why" behind user drop-offs.

## 1. Quantitative Analysis (GA4)
- **Funnel Exploration**: Where is the biggest percentage drop-off? (e.g., Landed -> Scrolled to CTA -> Opened Form -> Submitted).
- **Device Performance**: Is conversion significantly lower on Android vs. iOS?
- **Page Performance**: Which sections are being ignored?

## 2. Qualitative Analysis (Heatmaps & Recordings)
Tools: Microsoft Clarity, Hotjar.
- **Click Maps**: Are users clicking non-clickable elements?
- **Scroll Maps**: Where do most users stop reading? (Adjust `experience-architect` layout based on this).
- **Rage Clicks**: Identify broken UI or confusing UX.
- **Session Recordings**: Watch the "last 10 non-converters" to see where they got stuck.

## 3. Form Analysis
- **Time to Complete**: Is the form too long?
- **Field Drop-off**: Which specific field makes people leave?
- **Error Validation**: Are users struggling with specific input formats (e.g., Phone number)?

## 4. Heuristic Conversion Audit
- **Clarity**: Is the value prop clear in 5 seconds?
- **Relevance**: Does the page match the ad promise?
- **Anxiety**: Are there enough trust signals?
- **Distraction**: Are there too many links or buttons?
- **Urgency**: Is there a clear reason to act *now*?

## Research Handoff
Summarize findings into:
1. **The Observation**: (e.g., "60% of users drop off at the 'Phone Number' field.")
2. **The Hypothesis**: (e.g., "Asking for a phone number too early creates friction.")
3. **The Proposed Test**: (e.g., "Move the phone number field to the second step of the form.")
