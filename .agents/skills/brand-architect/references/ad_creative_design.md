# Ad Creative Design Guide

Designing high-impact visuals that drive clicks and maintain brand integrity.

## 1. Platform-Specific Design
- **Meta (FB/IG)**:
    - *Images*: High-contrast, single focal point, minimal text (keep under 20% for reach).
    - *Videos*: Vertical (9:16) for Reels/Stories. Hook within 2 seconds.
- **TikTok**:
    - *Style*: Must look native ("Lo-fi"). Use TikTok UI safe zones.
    - *Motion*: Fast cuts, text overlays, and high energy.
- **Google Display**:
    - *Clarity*: Must be clear even at small sizes. Strong brand logo placement.

## 2. Visual Hook Strategies
- **Emotional Connection**: Show real people experiencing the benefit.
- **The "Contrast" Strategy**: Use colors that stand out from the platform's UI (e.g., using brand colors on a white/light mode feed).
- **Social Proof Visuals**: Include 5-star ratings or "Trusted by" badges directly in the creative.
- **Outcome Visualization**: Show the "After" state (e.g., the customer enjoying the primary benefit of the product/service).

## 3. The "Scroll-Stop" Factor
- **Oddity/Curiosity**: Use a visual element that is slightly unexpected to stop the thumb.
- **Movement**: Use subtle animations or "Cinemagraphs" in static placements.
- **Faces**: Human faces (especially looking at the camera or the CTA) naturally attract attention.

## 4. Design-Copy Harmony
- **Message Match**: The primary visual must illustrate the primary headline.
- **Lead-In**: The visual should "point" or lead the eye toward the CTA button or text.
- **Hierarchy**: Ensure the headline is the most prominent text element in the design.

## 5. Visual A/B Testing Ideas
- **Personas**: Test a young couple vs. an elderly couple vs. a family.
- **Color Palettes**: Test the primary brand color vs. a high-contrast accent color.
- **Lifestyles**: Test a "Luxury/Premium" visual vs. an "Authentic/Spiritual" visual.
- **Formats**: Single image vs. Carousel vs. Short-form video.
